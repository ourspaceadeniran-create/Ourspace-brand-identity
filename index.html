Identity

Our Logo

The OurSpace mark is made of two strokes — a small upper loop and a wide lower sweep — forming an abstract heart, with a cross rising from the top. It represents covenant, faith, and the space two people share. It is never stretched, recoloured, or separated.

Type

Typographic System

Two typefaces. One carries elegance and soul — the other carries clarity and function. Together they create a voice that is both warm and credible, intimate and trustworthy.

Display / Serif
Headlines, pull quotes, brand wordmark, display text, section titles, poetry, scripture references.

Garamond
Body / Sans
Body copy, captions, labels, UI text, navigation, descriptions, episode notes.
DM Sans — clean, warm, and readable at every size. It never competes with Cormorant Garamond; it supports it. Use weight 300 for body, 400 for UI, 500 for labels only.

04

Tone

Brand Voice

OurSpace speaks like a trusted couple in your life — honest, warm, scripturally grounded, and never performative. We are not a church, a therapist, or a celebrity brand. We are real people sharing what we're learning.

We Are
  • Honest — We share our real experiences, including the hard ones. We don't present a highlight reel marriage.
  • Grounded in Scripture — The Word is our reference point, not opinion or popular culture. We exegete, we don't just quote.
  • Warm and Direct — We don't hedge endlessly. We say what we mean with kindness and conviction.
  • Culturally aware — We understand the Nigerian and African experience, and we speak to the global Christian couple without erasing that identity.
  • Purposeful — Every piece of content exists to strengthen marriages, not just to perform or entertain.
We Are Not
  • Preachy or moralistic — we don't lecture, we share and invite.
  • Overly formal or distant — we don't write like an institution.
  • Celebrity-adjacent — we don't use our platform to build personal fame.
  • Shallow or motivational-poster-only — depth matters; we go there.
  • Therapy — we refer couples to professional help when appropriate and never position ourselves as mental health practitioners.

Imagery Style

Photography Direction

01
Warm and Natural
Use warm-toned, naturally lit photography. No harsh studio lighting, no cold blue tones. The aesthetic is candid intimacy — couples in their real space.
02
Real Over Perfect
Prefer genuine moments over posed perfection. Laughing mid-sentence, thoughtful eye contact, quiet togetherness — these speak louder than curated beauty shots.
03
No Stock Faces
Never use generic stock couple photography. OurSpace imagery is always of real couples — ideally us, or with explicit permission from the couples featured.
